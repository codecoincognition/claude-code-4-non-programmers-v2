# Q2 Campaign Brief — Onboarding Refresh

Here's what the data said. New users who finish setup in under ten
minutes stick around. New users who don't, leave inside a week. The
line between the two groups is one screen — the integrations screen,
where forty percent of people stop.

So the campaign is about that one screen.

## The problem in one sentence

We ask people to connect five tools before they've seen a single
result. That's backwards. Nobody wires up their whole stack on faith.

## The fix

We move the payoff first. Show the user one finished report built
from sample data on screen two. Then ask for one integration, not
five. The other four can wait until they've felt the thing work.

## What we're shipping

Three emails, one in-app nudge, one short video. The video is sixty
seconds and it's a screen recording of the new flow with no voiceover
— just the screen doing the work. People believe what they watch more
than what they're told.

## The number we're chasing

Cut the integrations-screen drop-off from forty percent to twenty-five
by end of Q2. That's not a stretch goal. It's the floor.

## Risks

The biggest risk is scope creep. Every team wants their tool featured
on screen two. We feature one. The one with the highest connect rate
last quarter. The rest go in the "add more" tray.

Worth re-running this analysis in Q3 once the new flow has data.
