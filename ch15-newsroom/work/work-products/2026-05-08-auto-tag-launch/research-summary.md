## Past-launch patterns for auto-tag

Three most-similar past launches: smart-routing (Sep 2024),
auto-summary (Mar 2025), sentiment-tag (Nov 2025).

What landed across all three:
  • Customer story in the lead, not a feature description.
  • One quantified outcome anchored to user time saved
    (not throughput, not feature counts).
  • Quote from a beta customer in the middle, not at the end.

What didn't land:
  • Long technical preambles. smart-routing buried the customer
    payoff in paragraph 4 and got 30% lower CTR (click-through
    rate — the share of readers who clicked) than the others.
  • Adjectives in the headline ("powerful," "intelligent").
    auto-summary's first headline was "Powerful new summarization"
    and it underperformed the eventual headline by 2x.

Structural recommendation for this draft:
  1. Open with a specific support-team customer story.
  2. Anchor on activation time saved, not classification accuracy.
  3. Beta-customer quote in paragraph 3 of 5.
  4. Close with a question (not a CTA — a call to action, the
     "click here / sign up now" line).
