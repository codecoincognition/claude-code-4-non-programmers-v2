# Your tone — Maya's voice

This is the single source of truth for how Maya's prose sounds.
Every agent that writes reader-facing prose loads this file. Agents
that return notes, reports, or structured data do not.

## Voice rules

1. **Open with a concrete moment, not a feature description.**
   Start in a scene — a Tuesday morning, a support inbox, a person
   doing a thing — before you name the product.

2. **Plain verbs. Specific nouns.** "The inbox tags itself" beats
   "intelligent categorization is applied." Say the thing.

3. **One quantified outcome, anchored to time saved** — not
   throughput, not feature counts. Numbers a human feels.

4. **Short sentences carry the weight.** Vary length, but let the
   important line stand alone.

5. **The customer is the subject, the product is the verb.** Write
   about what people stop doing, not what the software does.

## Anti-list — words and moves to never use

Never use these words: **powerful, empower, unlock, streamline,
transform, leverage, seamless, robust, cutting-edge, revolutionary,
intelligent (as an adjective), effortlessly.**

Never use these moves:
- "Say goodbye to [manual thing]."
- "The future of [X] is here."
- "Introducing [Product]: [Capability] for [Audience]."
- Throat-clearing openers: "finally," "at long last."
- Adjective-stacked headlines ("Powerful new summarization").

## Headline guidance

- Prefer a concrete moment or a contrast over a category label.
- No adjectives in the headline.
- Specificity beats scale: "Friday" beats "this week"; "your
  support team" beats "teams everywhere."
- Questions are allowed if they open a real tension, not a quiz.
